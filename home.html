Organizational Directives

Mission

Our mission is to advocate for the needs and rights of children with neurological conditions and provide psychosocial support for parents, ensuring their well-being as they care for these special children.

Objectives

Our objectives are to mobilize resources to develop a school for neurodiverse children, advocate for their rights and needs, provide psychosocial support for caregivers, promote recreational activities, ensure access to affordable therapies and assistive devices, foster mutual support among members, and encourage participation in group initiatives like diaper drives.

Vision

"To build a global community that increases the inclusion and celebration of neurodiversity"

Tri-Cycles

SFN is dedicated to empowering individuals with disabilities by providing essential resources, including wheelchairs. These mobility aids are transformative, granting access to education, employment opportunities, and social engagement. By equipping individuals with the freedom to move independently, SFN contributes to a more inclusive and equitable society where everyone can reach their full potential.

SFN provides wheelchairs as part of its commitment to enhancing mobility and independence for individuals with disabilities. These essential tools open doors to education, employment, and social participation, empowering beneficiaries to live more fulfilling lives.

Diapers

SFN recognizes the unique challenges faced by caregivers of children with disabilities. Providing diapers is a crucial aspect of our support services. By supplying these essential items, we alleviate financial burdens and enable caregivers to focus on their child's overall well-being.

This support contributes to improved hygiene, health, and quality of life for both the child and the family.

SFN recognizes the unique challenges faced by caregivers of children with disabilities.

White cane, Malacca cane, Wheel Chairs

SFN is committed to enhancing the lives of people with visual impairments. We provide essential tools like white canes and Malacca canes to foster independence and mobility. These walking aids are crucial for navigating the world safely and confidently.

By equipping individuals with these resources, we aim to create a more inclusive society where everyone can participate fully.
